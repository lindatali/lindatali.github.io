Project Context

Rimowa, the iconic LVMH-owned luxury luggage brand, partnered with Threedium to bring their entire product catalog to life through interactive 3D and AR experiences on their website. But Threedium specialized in animation and real-time rendering‚Äînot base 3D modeling. That's where Alpha3D came in.

As Project Manager at Alpha3D, I was tasked with coordinating a complex three-party collaboration: Rimowa (client), Threedium (animation partner), and Alpha3D (modeling provider). The challenge? Deliver animation-ready 3D models of Rimowa's bags and suitcases that met both Threedium's technical specifications and Rimowa's exacting luxury standards.

Why This Project Was Technically Complex

üéØ The Unique Challenge

This wasn't standard 3D modeling. Rimowa's products needed to be animated‚Äîsuitcases opening, handles extending, wheels rotating. That meant our models required:

  • Proper Topology: Clean edge loops for deformation without artifacts
  • Modular Construction: Separate components (body, handle, wheels, zippers) that could be individually animated
  • Rigging-Ready Geometry: Models structured for Threedium's animation team to add bones and controls
  • 1:1 Accuracy: Exact physical dimensions and proportions of real Rimowa products
  • Luxury-Grade Materials: Realistic textures for aluminum grooves, leather handles, polycarbonate shells

Most 3D modeling work is "hero assets"‚Äîstatic, beautiful objects for product pages. This required deep coordination between modelers (Alpha3D), animators (Threedium), and brand stakeholders (Rimowa) to ensure our deliverables would actually work downstream.

My Role & Responsibilities

Three-Party Coordination

I served as the central coordination point between three organizations with different priorities:

  • Rimowa: Brand standards, approval workflows, product reference materials, feedback cycles
  • Threedium: Technical specifications, file format requirements, animation needs, quality gates
  • Alpha3D: Modeling team allocation, production timelines, QA processes, budget management

Workflow Documentation & Process Design

üìã Critical Early Investment

Before starting production, I invested heavily in documenting workflows and establishing communication protocols. This upfront work was essential because:

  • Teams were in different time zones (Estonia, UK, Germany)
  • Handoffs between Alpha3D and Threedium needed to be seamless

  • Multiple iterations were expected‚Äîwe needed efficient revision cycles

I created detailed documentation covering:

  • Model delivery specifications (polygon counts, UV unwrapping, material channels)

  • Feedback loops and revision request procedures
  • Escalation paths for technical blockers

Production Process

1

Kickoff & Requirements Gathering

Facilitated joint kickoff call with all three parties. Collected Rimowa product references (photos, CAD files, physical measurements). Documented Threedium's technical requirements for animation-ready models.

2

Pilot Asset Production

Selected one representative suitcase as pilot. Alpha3D modeled it according to specs. Sent to Threedium for animation test. Gathered feedback on what worked and what needed adjustment. Refined workflow based on learnings.

3

Team Allocation & Training

Assigned multiple specialized teams at Alpha3D‚Äîone focused on hard-surface modeling (suitcase bodies), another on soft goods (leather handles, straps). Conducted training session on Threedium's technical requirements to ensure consistency.

4

Scaled Production

Moved into full production across Rimowa's product line. Maintained weekly check-ins with Threedium on quality and technical fit. Coordinated feedback from Rimowa on brand accuracy. Managed parallel workstreams to meet aggressive timeline.

5

Rigorous QA & Handoff

Every model went through multi-stage QA: (1) Alpha3D internal review, (2) technical validation against Threedium specs, (3) visual review by Rimowa stakeholders. Only after all three approvals did models move to Threedium for animation.

6

Post-Delivery Support

Remained available for revision requests as Threedium began animation work. Some models needed topology adjustments when deformation revealed issues. Quick turnaround on fixes maintained project momentum.

Key Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Strict Quality Control

The Problem: Rimowa is an LVMH brand‚Äîquality standards are non-negotiable. Models needed to be pixel-perfect representations of iconic designs. Any visible imperfections would be rejected.

My Solution: Implemented multi-stakeholder QA process where models couldn't proceed without sign-off from Alpha3D, Threedium, AND Rimowa. This caught issues early before they cascaded downstream. Also allocated only senior artists to project‚Äîno junior team members who might miss details.

Challenge 2: Complex Multi-Team Sync

The Problem: Three companies across different time zones needed tight coordination. Miscommunication could derail timelines.

My Solution: Established clear communication protocols‚Äîweekly alignment calls, shared Slack channel for quick questions, centralized file repository with version control. I served as single point of contact to prevent conflicting instructions reaching my team.

Challenge 3: Animation Requirements Learning Curve

The Problem: Alpha3D's team was excellent at static 3D modeling but less experienced with animation-ready topology. First models had issues when Threedium tried to animate them.

My Solution: Used pilot asset as teaching opportunity. Gathered detailed feedback from Threedium on what worked/didn't work. Conducted training session with team on edge loop placement, modular construction, and animation-friendly geometry. Quality improved dramatically after first few assets.

Key Takeaways

1. Documentation Upfront Saves Time Later

The days spent documenting workflows, technical specs, and communication protocols felt tedious but paid massive dividends. Clear documentation prevented misunderstandings that would have caused costly rework.

2. Pilot Assets De-Risk Complex Projects

Testing end-to-end workflow on one asset before scaling production revealed issues we could fix early. Without the pilot, we would have discovered problems after modeling 50+ suitcases‚Äîcatastrophic waste.

3. Multi-Party Projects Need Single Point of Contact

By serving as the coordination hub between Rimowa, Threedium, and Alpha3D, I prevented conflicting instructions and kept everyone aligned. Distributed coordination would have created chaos.

4. Invest in Team Training for New Domains

Our team was strong at static modeling but needed education on animation requirements. Rather than hoping they'd figure it out, I facilitated training based on Threedium's feedback. Capability investment paid off immediately.
